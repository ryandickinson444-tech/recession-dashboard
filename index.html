Who the market actually measures

The index is essentially a wealth gauge for the top decile. A record S&P sitting next to crisis-level consumer sentiment and rising credit-card delinquency is the K-shaped economy in one frame — the market under-reports both the wealth concentrated at the top and the strain building at the bottom, which historically gets hit hardest through jobs and credit, not stock prices.

Methodology & honest caveats

Not a forecast, not market timing. This counts stretched conditions; it cannot tell you when. Expensive markets can stay expensive for years — CAPE and Buffett have flashed "expensive" since roughly 2017.

These signals lie sometimes. The yield curve re-steepens before recessions but also head-fakes. The LEI signaled a 2024 recession that never came. The Sahm Rule briefly, falsely tripped in 2024. Trust the cluster and the trend, not any single light.

The tension right now is valuation and monetary (very stretched, hawkish Fed, sticky ~4.2% inflation, a live oil shock) versus the hard data (Sahm benign, claims healthy, LEI positive). Prices are priced for perfection while the Fed can't ease — that's the fragility, more than any single recession trigger.
